About Kyoto

Kyoto, located in the Kansai region of Japan, is renowned for its historical significance, traditional tea houses, exquisite gardens, and breathtaking temples. As the former capital of Japan for over a thousand years, Kyoto is home to 17 UNESCO World Heritage Sites, making it one of Japan’s most culturally rich cities.

Top Attractions

  • Fushimi Inari Taisha: Famous for its iconic thousands of red torii gates that lead up Mount Inari, offering a spiritual and scenic journey.
  • Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion): This Zen Buddhist temple is known for its stunning golden exterior, beautifully reflected in the surrounding pond.
  • Arashiyama Bamboo Grove: A mesmerizing bamboo forest that creates a unique atmosphere for a peaceful stroll. It's one of Kyoto’s most iconic spots.
  • Gion District: A traditional geisha district, where you can still witness the beauty of ancient tea houses and glimpse geishas on their way to appointments.

Unique Experiences

  • Tea Ceremony: Kyoto is famous for its tea culture, and experiencing a traditional Japanese tea ceremony is a must-do to understand the heart of Japanese hospitality.
  • Kimono Wearing: Experience the elegance of Kyoto by dressing in a kimono and strolling through the historic streets and temples of the city.
  • Zen Garden Meditation: Visit one of Kyoto’s serene Zen gardens, such as those at Kinkaku-ji or Ryoan-ji, to practice meditation and find inner peace.

Local Flavors

Kyoto’s cuisine is deeply connected to its traditions and nature. The city’s culinary offerings are based on seasonal ingredients, simple yet exquisite flavors, and centuries-old recipes. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Kaiseki: A traditional multi-course meal that emphasizes seasonal ingredients and presentation, often served in the best restaurants and tea houses in Kyoto.
  • Yudofu: A hot pot dish made with tofu, commonly served at Buddhist temples in Kyoto. It’s light yet rich in flavor.
  • Kyo-wagashi: Traditional Japanese sweets often made with sweet red bean paste, perfect for pairing with matcha tea during a tea ceremony.
