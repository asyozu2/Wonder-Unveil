About Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu, an ancient Inca city perched high in the Andes Mountains of Peru, is one of the most iconic and mysterious archaeological sites in the world. Built in the 15th century and later abandoned, it remained hidden from the outside world until its rediscovery in 1911. Today, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

Top Attractions

  • The Inca Trail: A famous trekking route leading to Machu Picchu, offering spectacular views of the Andes and passing through stunning Incan ruins.
  • The Sun Gate (Inti Punku): A viewpoint at the end of the Inca Trail, offering panoramic views of Machu Picchu as the sun rises over the ancient citadel.
  • The Temple of the Sun: A circular structure at Machu Picchu that is aligned with the sun during the solstices, reflecting the Incan’s advanced understanding of astronomy.
  • Intihuatana Stone: A ritual stone associated with astronomical and religious ceremonies, believed to have been used by the Incas to track the movement of the sun.

Unique Experiences

  • Sunrise at Machu Picchu: Watching the first rays of sunlight illuminate the ancient city is a once-in-a-lifetime experience, offering a sense of awe and wonder.
  • Trekking the Inca Trail: Embark on a multi-day trek through stunning landscapes, passing through Incan ruins, lush cloud forests, and picturesque villages, before reaching Machu Picchu.
  • Cultural Tours: Take guided tours with local historians and guides who provide deep insights into the history, culture, and engineering marvels of the Inca civilization.

Local Flavors

Peruvian cuisine is diverse and vibrant, reflecting the country's rich cultural heritage. In the area around Machu Picchu, you'll find traditional Andean dishes that have been passed down through generations:

  • Ceviche: Fresh fish marinated in citrus juices, typically served with onions, cilantro, and chili, is a staple of Peruvian coastal cuisine.
  • Cuy: Roasted guinea pig, a traditional Andean dish often enjoyed during festivals and special occasions.
  • Trucha (Trout): Often served in the Sacred Valley, this fish is typically pan-fried and seasoned with local herbs and spices.
  • Chicha Morada: A sweet beverage made from purple corn, often flavored with cinnamon and cloves, and served cold as a refreshing drink.
