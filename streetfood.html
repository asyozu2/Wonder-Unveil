Street Food Diaries: Manila Edition

A journey through Manila's vibrant and flavorful street food scene.

Manila, the bustling heart of the Philippines, is not just a city of vibrant culture and rich history, but also a haven for street food lovers. As the sun sets and the streets come alive with the hum of activity, the city's street food vendors set up their stalls, offering an irresistible array of savory, sweet, and savory-sweet snacks.

1. Isaw (Grilled Chicken Intestines)

Isaw is a Filipino street food classic that’s bound to make any food lover’s taste buds tingle. The chicken intestines are marinated, skewered, and grilled to perfection, delivering a smoky flavor with a slightly crispy exterior and a juicy interior...

2. Balut (Fertilized Duck Egg)

One of Manila’s most controversial street foods, balut, is both beloved and feared by locals and tourists alike...

3. Kwek-Kwek (Quail Eggs in Batter)

Small but mighty, kwek-kwek are hard-boiled quail eggs dipped in orange-colored batter and deep-fried to a crunchy perfection...

4. Fish Balls and Squid Balls

No Manila street food experience is complete without fish balls and squid balls. These spherical delights are made from fish or squid paste, molded into balls, and deep-fried until golden brown...

5. Banana Cue (Caramelized Fried Banana)

For those with a sweet tooth, banana cue is a must-try street snack. Slices of saba bananas (a local variety) are deep-fried in hot oil until golden brown, then coated in caramelized brown sugar...

6. Adidas (Grilled Chicken Feet)

Yes, you read that correctly—adidas is a Filipino street food made from grilled chicken feet. Despite its unorthodox appearance, adidas has a devoted following in Manila...

7. Betamax (Grilled coagulated chicken or pork blood)

Named after the old Betamax video cassette tapes, this unique snack is made from coagulated chicken or pork blood...

8. Turon (Sweet Banana Spring Rolls)

Turon is a sweet and crispy Filipino snack made from saba bananas, sometimes with a slice of jackfruit, wrapped in a spring roll wrapper, and fried until golden...

9. Manggang hilaw (Green Mango with Bagoong)

For something more refreshing, Manggang hilaw is the Filipino answer to a tangy, salty snack...

10. Taho (Sweet Silken Tofu)

Taho is a warm, comforting snack that’s especially popular during the early mornings. It consists of silken tofu topped with syrupy brown sugar and a generous amount of sago pearls...

The Culture of Manila’s Street Food

Street food in Manila is more than just a quick snack—it’s a way of life. The vendors who sell these treats have become a part of the city’s vibrant rhythm...
