Machu Picchu: Lost City of the Incas

An ancient citadel perched high in the Andes, Machu Picchu offers breathtaking views and rich Inca history.

About Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu, built in the 15th century and rediscovered in 1911, stands as one of the world's most remarkable archaeological sites. Nestled at 2,430 m above sea level above the Urubamba Valley, it showcases sophisticated Inca stonework and panoramic mountain vistas.

Top Attractions

  • The Citadel: Explore terraced fields, plazas, and temples arranged along the Andean ridge.
  • Temple of the Sun: A semicircular sanctuary used for astronomical observations and solar ceremonies.
  • Intihuatana Stone: The carved “hitching post of the sun” calendar stone, central to Inca rituals.
  • Temple of Three Windows: Framing the sunrise, these trapezoidal windows offer both beauty and cosmological significance.
  • Sacred Plaza: The ceremonial heart surrounded by the Main Temple and other key structures.
  • Inca Bridge: A cliffside stone path once used to guard the main approach to the citadel.
  • Royal Tomb: Subterranean chamber beneath the Temple of the Sun, showcasing precise Inca masonry.

Must-Do Hikes

  • Huayna Picchu: Steep, rewarding trail up the “young peak” for iconic overhead views of the ruins.
  • Machu Picchu Mountain: Wider switchbacks and fewer crowds lead to sweeping panoramas of the site and valley below.
  • Sun Gate (Inti Punku): The classic finish to the Inca Trail, revealing Machu Picchu at dawn.

The Gateway Town

Aguas Calientes: Also called Machu Picchu Pueblo, this riverside town is your base for trains, buses, lodging, and relaxing in its natural hot springs.

Helpful Tips

  • Book entry tickets and bus/train in advance—the site limits daily visitors.
  • Pack layers and rain gear: mountain weather can shift quickly.
  • Stay hydrated and take breaks—altitude often slows you down.
  • Respect the ruins: no climbing on walls, and follow all park guidelines.
  • Consider spending a night in Aguas Calientes to acclimate and catch sunrise at the Sun Gate.
