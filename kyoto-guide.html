Kyoto: Ancient Capital of Japan

A journey through Kyoto’s timeless temples, serene gardens, and rich cultural traditions.

About Kyoto

Kyoto served as Japan’s imperial capital for over a thousand years and remains the heart of traditional Japanese culture. With hundreds of shrines, temples, and historic districts, Kyoto offers a glimpse of old-world Japan alongside its modern facets.

Top Attractions

  • Fushimi Inari Taisha: Walk through endless red torii gates up Mount Inari—a spiritual and photo-worthy experience.
  • Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion): Admire the stunning gold-leaf covered temple reflecting on a tranquil pond.
  • Kiyomizu-dera: Stand on the wooden stage overlooking cherry-blossom and maple-filled hillsides.
  • Arashiyama Bamboo Grove: Wander among towering bamboo stalks and feel the peaceful hush of the grove.

Best Experiences

  • Traditional Tea Ceremony in Gion: Participate in a matcha tea ceremony in a historic teahouse.
  • Stroll the Philosopher’s Path: Walk the cherry-lined canal trail, especially beautiful in spring.
  • Geisha & Maiko Spotting: Explore Hanamikoji Street and Pontocho Alley at dusk for a chance to see geiko and maiko.

Local Cuisine

  • Kaiseki Ryōri: Multi-course haute-cuisine meal featuring seasonal, artfully presented dishes.
  • Yudōfu: Silken tofu simmered in a light broth—especially popular near Zen temples.
  • Matcha Sweets: Green tea–flavored desserts like ice cream, cakes, and traditional wagashi.

Helpful Tips for Travelers

To make the most of your Kyoto visit:

  • Purchase an IC card (ICOCA) for easy bus and train rides.
  • Wear comfortable shoes—many streets and shrines involve steep paths or gravel.
  • Respect temple etiquette: bow at the torii, cleanse at the chōzuya, and speak quietly.
  • Consider renting a bicycle for scenic rides along the Kamo River or through residential streets.
  • Check opening hours and reservation requirements for popular temples and tea ceremonies.
