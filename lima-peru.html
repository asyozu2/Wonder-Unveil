Lima: Peru’s Gastronomic & Cultural Capital

A sprawling coastal city where colonial history, world-class cuisine, and Pacific Ocean sunsets converge.

About Lima

Founded in 1535 by the Spanish conquistador Francisco Pizarro, Lima is Peru’s capital and largest city. Nestled along the Pacific coast, it blends elegant colonial architecture with vibrant modern districts, earning its reputation as the “City of Kings.”

Top Attractions

  • Plaza Mayor: The historic heart of Lima, surrounded by the Presidential Palace, Cathedral, and ornate colonial arcades.
  • Miraflores Boardwalk (Malecón): Clifftop parks overlooking the Pacific—perfect for sunset strolls and paragliding.
  • Barranco District: Lima’s bohemian neighborhood, full of street art, live music venues, and the iconic Puente de los Suspiros.
  • Museo Larco: Housed in an 18th-century vice-royal mansion, this museum showcases pre-Columbian art and the famous erotic pottery gallery.

Must-Try Culinary Experiences

  • Ceviche: Peru’s national dish—fresh raw fish cured in lime juice, tossed with onions, cilantro, and aji pepper.
  • Lomo Saltado: Stir-fried beef with onions, tomatoes, and fries, served over rice—a fusion of Chinese and Peruvian flavors.
  • Anticuchos: Grilled beef heart skewers, slathered in spicy marinades and served with boiled potatoes and corn.
  • Chocolates & Pisco Sours: Sample artisanal chocolates in Barranco and sip the classic Pisco Sour at a historic bar in Miraflores.

Neighborhood Highlights & Experiences

  • Magic Water Circuit: Parque de la Reserva’s illuminated fountains and nightly light shows.
  • Parque Kennedy: The bustling heart of Miraflores—street vendors, cat lounges, and open-air cafes.
  • Historic Churches: Explore Santo Domingo and San Francisco with their catacombs and Baroque interiors.
  • Surf in Costa Verde: Catch beginner waves off Miraflores or watch the pros at Punta Roquitas.

Helpful Tips

  • Carry small bills and coins for taxis, street snacks, and market purchases.
  • Stay aware of your belongings in crowded areas—petty theft can occur.
  • Avoid drinking tap water; bottled water is inexpensive and widely available.
  • Use Uber or registered taxis after dark, especially in unfamiliar districts.
  • Plan visits to museums and fountains in advance—some require timed tickets.
